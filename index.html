What you need to know

Welcome to the uOttawa Engineering Mini Eco-Marathon. This event will challenge the minds of young students and introduce them to the field of engineering. As the future of the automotive industry heads towards electricity, we hope to open young minds to the concepts of electric automobiles and efficiency. Additionally, this event will provide students with a chance to build their technical skills while working together as a team towards a common goal. The fun-filled event consists of a full day of racing and competition, with a break for lunch, followed by vehicle presentations. Additionally, teams will be ranked based on a points system and the highest-scoring teams will receive awards and prizes. Teams will be competing for the most efficient, the fastest in a drag race, engineering design, media, best-looking car, and presentations. This encourages the teams to have various skills such as artistic, public speaking, marketing, and overall engineering. Overall, this event will challenge students in fun and competitive environment where their skills will be tested, and teamwork will be necessary. Come to the uOttawa Engineering Mini Eco Marathon, and join us on our mission to improve electric vehicle technology for future generations!

FAQ

What are the different competition categories?

With our Mini Eco-Marathon, your team is going to be competing in multiple exciting challenges, which allow you to compete in efficiency, speed, engineering design, media, best-looking car, presentation categories.

To find out the specific rules for these various competitions, check out our rules.

What do teams need to bring to the competition?

Teams can be a group of up to 5 students. All you need to bring is your car, tools, waivers and your enthusiasm. Get ready for a day filled with excitement!

Where will the competition be held?

The competition will be held on Saturday, April 27, 2019, at the uOttawa STEM Complex. Hope to see you there!

How many teams can a high school register

This entire competition was put into place for high school students to be as creative as possible with their innovations. All high schools are welcome and any high school can register multiple teams!

How long does registration last?

We want registration to be open to all high schools as long as possible. Our registration will last until April 10, 2017.

What do you win!

The greatest thing that any student can ask for is the chance to create new projects and make experiences with current uOttawa students and professors.

At the uOttawa Engineering Mini Eco-Marathon, students will be ranked on the competitions and have the chance to win various prizes, including scholarships to uOttawa Engineering for the winning team members upon graduating from high school.
